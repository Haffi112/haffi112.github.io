Authorship convention

In NLP and ML venues, last author typically denotes the senior/PI position; first author is the lead executor (often a student I supervise). In cardiology, food science, and human genetics, the author position has different meanings — typically lead author is the primary investigator and middle authors contribute data, methods, or analysis.

Computational Neuroscience Selected

Bootstrap percolation with inhibition

Einarsson, H., Lengler, J., Mousset, F., Panagiotou, K., Steger, A. · Random Structures & Algorithms · 2019

Studies bootstrap percolation on random graphs in the presence of inhibitory vertices, a generalisation of the classical activation cascade model motivated by cortical excitation-inhibition balance. Introduces a probabilistic framework that captures how the addition of inhibition shifts the activation threshold and the size of the final active set. Published in Random Structures & Algorithms, a premier journal in probabilistic combinatorics; one of the key results of the author's PhD.

Applications of BERT models towards automation of clinical coding in icelandic

Hauksson, H. O., Einarsson, H. · Findings of the association for computational linguistics: NAACL 2024 · 2024

Applies BERT-style models to automate ICD-10 clinical coding in Icelandic hospital records, treating coding as a hierarchical multi-label classification problem. Bootstrap percolation with inhibition

Einarsson, H., Lengler, J., Mousset, F., Panagiotou, K., Steger, A. · Random Structures & Algorithms · 2019

Studies bootstrap percolation on random graphs in the presence of inhibitory vertices, a generalisation of the classical activation cascade model motivated by cortical excitation-inhibition balance. Introduces a probabilistic framework that captures how the addition of inhibition shifts the activation threshold and the size of the final active set. Published in Random Structures & Algorithms, a premier journal in probabilistic combinatorics; one of the key results of the author's PhD.
